Know Your Enemy

The Problem

Existing smart watch calendar applications are lacking. They cannot add events to the calendar or edit them. If they can do so, the typing ability is horrendous. These applications are difficult to read and difficult to use for many.

The Customer

People who have a smart watch that like to keep track of events on their phone or Google account. See 'Customers' under the stakeholders section.

Scope

We will design an interface for a new application that could be installed instead of the existing calendar application. This application will only be designed for square Android wear smartwatches and we will not be designing ways in which the application could interact with other aspects of the watch OS (notifications, etc.)

Stakeholders

The most obvious stakeholder for any project is us, the team designing the application. Our goal is to make a viable design that pleases potential customers, in addition to being helpful and simple enough to implement in further stages of development. We must, however, be wary that our design does not take us too long to come up with, lest the project may become too costly.

Another potential group of stakeholders is the rest of the team that is a part of our project. This would include project managers, any developers that plan to use our design, and even any salespeople that rely on the overall success of the product. All of this group hopes that the application is easy and enjoyable to use and is completed in a minimal amount of time. They worry that our design may take too long to implement and that the application as a whole won't be well-recieved by customers. We can continue to involve them by continually showing them our prototypes as they are designed and requesting feedback based on their perspective.

Customers

There are a few different types of customers we expect to take interest in our app. Managers, who tend to have busy schedules with lots of meetings. Students, who would use the calendar to plan for their classes. And parents, who may use teh calendar to help them rembemer when and where they need to bring their children. Since the app is for a smartwatch, we can safely assume that most cusomters will already be well aquianted with technology.

Manager's want their calendar app to discretely remind them when they have somewhere to be. They would want to be reminded even if they are currently in a meeting, and unable to check their phone. They would worry that constantly checking their watch may make the person they're meeting with feel rushed, and less of a priority. As with all customers, the best way to involve them will be by allowing them to test the prototypes as they come along, so their feedback can be implemented.

Students need their calendar app to be aggressive. Students often have a reputation of sleeping too long and being late to their engagements as a result. Students may feel time pressure from the piling assignments in their classes, and would turn to a calendar app to improve their time management. Students worry that if their calendar app is not reliable, they may want more feedback to be sure that an event has been correctly added.

Parents are busy. They want to be able to add an event quickly, and if they have young children, then they will often be in a loud environment and incabable of relying on speech to text. Beyond that, they will share many of the desires and concerns of the other two groups.

Prototyping

We will prototype adding an event, viewing an existing event, editing an event, and deleting an event. These are important aspects to prototype because they showcase the main functionality of the application and all of the unique pieces of the design. So, other areas of the application that will not be shows will reuse parts of the design shown in these prototypes.

Why Prototype?

If we were to jump headfirst into creating this calendar application without first prototyping a solution, we may end up with a product that is difficult to use and which does not meet the needs of all our users. By prototyping a solution, we are able to test it and then iterate on the prototype until we've arrived at a final product worth creating.

Pain Points

  • Typing to add or edit an event
  • Viewing a month at a time
  • Being able to interact with events as needed
