Shoot 'em Up

I worked in a group of 4 to make a multiplayer videogame that allows any of the users to host the server on their own machines.

Our group met every week to discuss what each of our responsibilities would be for the following week and to talk about the progress we made. Unfortunately, I no longer have any of the sketches we made during these meetings.

Overall, the project was a terrific success! We met all of our goals well ahead of time, and we were able to incorporate added functionality, like in game terrain, and chat commands.

This project started because my group wanted to do more than was required to do well on the assignment. Instead we met frequently, and divided the labor in a way that allowed us to get significantly more done

The Biggest Challenge

At one point, about after we had integrated the server, the game started being very laggy. At the time, we were sending information to the client every game tick. After a while, I realized that instead, we could just send the trajectory of each object, rather than the object itself. That let us do server updates far less frequently, after that all the server communication ran smoothly.
